Dina Fleischman is wrong about animal harm

In her latest preprint, Fleischman argues that eating beef is more acceptable than eating chicken, because per unit of meat more animals suffer in the production of chicken. However, she does not account for externalities caused by cattle production. It is well-known that cows have far greater--and more diverse--emissions than chickens per unit of meat.[0]

Emissions are known to disrupt regular environmental patterns, such that phenomena like heat waves and reproductive cycle disruption become more common.[1] The worst recent example of this was when an estimated 10 billion Alaskan snow crabs died of starvation, reported by fishermen and confirmed by scientists in 2022.[2] This was caused by a massive heat wave sweeping through the Bering coast. Presumably, dying of starvation is an immense suffering for these crabs--Fleischman herself makes the assumption that the animals she examined, which include fish, have the same degree of suffering. Although one cannot directly link cattle consumption to the death of these crabs, when aggregating all death and suffering of all animals caused by irregular climate-related phenomena, one can easily draw the conclusion that cattle farming contributes more to this problem than chicken farming.

One externality that can be linked directly to cattle production is loss of habitat. Loss of habitat produces suffering and death in animals. By far, cattle require the most land and economic inputs of any domesticated animal. This has ramifications for the animals living in land deforested to make space or inputs for cattle. Orangutan are nearing extinction in part due to Indonesian cattle farming and farming of cattle feed.[3a][3b] The Amazon rainforest is depleting in part due to cattle farming and the Amazon has by far the most biodiversity of any contiguous piece of land.[4] Brazil--where Fleischman grew up--is in the top 5 highest rates of beef consumption in the world and the 3rd highest per capita. It also has the highest rate of deforestation, with Indonesia second.[5] Its neighbour Argentina is the highest consumer of beef of any country.[6]

Measuring the true scale of suffering caused by beef consumption is impossible. Countless animals have had to suffer or die because of deforestation and countless more have died or suffered due to emissions and climate change. Just by considering the 10b snow crabs alone, we see that an immense amount of suffering was caused for meat that was never consumed by humans. Using Diana's table, we can estimate their suffering. These snow crabs are not farmed and thus their days in captivity are negligible, but starvation takes months and is presumably worse than taking a bullet to the head or living in a small cell, so the suffering per day of life is probably higher than 4. Their weight is minimal and was not even consumed, which causes the "Adjusted days of suffering caused per kg demanded" as per Diana's table to skyrocket. 10 billion of them died, which is 1/3rd of the total number of chickens farmed.[7] Putting this together, we can see that the suffering of snow crabs caused by a heat wave, to which beef production probably contributed the most, is approximately "a lot."
